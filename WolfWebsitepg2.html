Etymology of the word wolf


The English "wolf" stems from the Old English wulf, which is itself thought to be derived from the Proto-Germanic. The Proto-Indo-European root may also be the source of the Latin word for the animal lupus The name "grey wolf" refers to the greyish colour of the species. Since pre-Christian times, Germanic peoples such as the Anglo-Saxons took on wulf as a prefix or suffix in their names. Examples include

  • Wulfhere ("Wolf Army")
  • Cynewulf ("Royal Wolf")
  • Cēnwulf ("Bold Wolf")
  • Æðelwulf ("Noble Wolf")
